FairSSL: Fair Multimodal Self-Supervised Learning

Jiaee Cheong*1,2,3, Abtin Mogharabin*4,5, Paul Pu Liang3, Hatice Gunes2, Sinan Kalkan6
1Harvard University, 2University of Cambridge, 3MIT, 4University of Tübingen, 5Zuse School ELIZA, 6METU-ROMER
*Equal contribution · ICML 2026
Paper Poster Code BibTeX

FairSSL uses subject-aware and modality-aware regularization for heterogeneous, variable-length, non-tabular multimodal data.

Abstract

Prevalent multimodal self-supervised learning (SSL) methods rely on the redundancy assumption: that different views share substantial task-relevant information. This assumption fails in complex, real-world settings characterized by heterogeneity, such as variable-length healthcare or behavioral data, where enforcing strict alignment can discard unique, modality-specific signals and inadvertently amplify bias.

FairSSL leverages data heterogeneity as a resource for fairness rather than a hindrance. Unlike standard contrastive approaches, FairSSL uses a subject-aware Variance-Invariance-Covariance Regularization objective, where alignment is enforced across segments drawn from the same subject. A segment-based pooling strategy handles variable-length modalities, while the objective encourages within-subject variability, cross-modal and cross-subject invariance, and representation decorrelation.

Theoretical analysis shows that the objective bounds the score gap between protected groups. Empirically, FairSSL improves fairness on heterogeneous multimodal datasets without sacrificing downstream predictive performance.

Method Summary

Motivation

Multimodal SSL is often designed around direct alignment between views. This works best when the views share substantial task-relevant information. In heterogeneous settings, modalities can have different lengths, temporal structures, and modality-specific signals.

Direct alignment can then suppress useful modality-specific information. FairSSL instead treats heterogeneity as structure that can support fairer multimodal representation learning.

Variable-Length Inputs

Each subject can have a different number of segments in each modality. One-to-one segment alignment is therefore not always well-defined.

FairSSL encodes each segment, pools one modality into a subject-level representation, and aligns the pooled representation with available segments from the other modality.

FairSSL Overview

Segment encoding and pooling

Each segment is encoded with a modality-specific encoder. FairSSL then average-pools one modality into a subject-level vector and aligns it against segment-level features from the other modality. This makes cross-modal alignment well-defined even when modalities have different numbers of segments.

VICReg components

Variance regularization prevents representation collapse. Covariance regularization reduces redundancy by decorrelating embedding dimensions. Invariance regularization encourages paired views to remain close in representation space.

FairSSL invariance variants

The four variants differ only in how the second-modality segments are selected for alignment. M1 aligns each subject with itself. M2 aligns a subject with all subjects in the batch. M3 uses same-class inter-subject alignment. M4 alternates between M2 and M3 across epochs.

Why FairSSL Improves Fairness

FairSSL links its regularization terms to fairness through group score behavior. The subject-aware invariance term reduces between-group score disparity, while the regularization terms jointly control within-group score spread.

In the statistical parity setting, the key takeaway is that fairness deviation is bounded by between-group score disparity together with within-group score spread.

\[ \left|R_{\mathrm{SP}} - 1\right| \leq O\!\left(\Delta_g(w)\right) + O\!\left(v_0(w) + v_1(w)\right) \]

The centroid-distance behavior is consistent with FairSSL reducing the separation between protected groups in the learned representation space.

Main Results

FairSSL improves the fairness-performance trade-off over strong SSL baselines across heterogeneous multimodal settings. The D-Vlog Pareto plot shows FairSSL variants moving the frontier toward higher fairness and stronger predictive performance.

Overall, FairSSL adapts SSL to heterogeneous, variable-length multimodal data, makes cross-modal alignment well-defined through pooling, and improves fairness without requiring strict segment matching.
